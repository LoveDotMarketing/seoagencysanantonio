FAQ

PPC & Digital Advertising Questions

How much should I budget for PPC?

Most San Antonio businesses start with $1,500 to $5,000 per month in ad spend, plus management fees. The optimal budget depends on your industry, local competition, and growth goals. High-competition verticals like legal services, medical practices, and home services typically require higher budgets to compete effectively. We recommend starting with a focused campaign on your highest-priority service, proving ROI, and then scaling spend into additional campaigns as results justify the investment.

What's a good ROAS for digital advertising?

A strong return on ad spend varies by industry and business model. E-commerce businesses typically target a 4:1 ROAS or higher, meaning four dollars of revenue for every dollar spent on ads. Lead generation businesses often measure success through cost per lead and customer lifetime value rather than direct ROAS. For most San Antonio service businesses, we aim for a 3:1 to 5:1 ROAS. During your onboround, we establish clear ROAS benchmarks based on your specific margins and customer value.

Should I use Google Ads or Facebook Ads?

It depends on your business model and target audience. Google Ads captures high-intent users who are actively searching for your services — making it ideal for service businesses, B2B companies, and anyone targeting customers with immediate purchase intent. Meta Ads (Facebook and Instagram) excel at building awareness, reaching new audiences based on interests and demographics, and retargeting past website visitors. Most San Antonio businesses benefit from both platforms working together in a coordinated strategy. We analyze your market and recommend the optimal channel mix based on where your customers actually spend their time.

How quickly will I see results from PPC?

Unlike SEO, PPC can generate traffic and leads within days of launching. However, optimizing campaigns for peak performance typically takes 30 to 90 days. During this period, we are gathering conversion data, testing ad variations, refining audience targeting, adjusting bidding strategies, and building negative keyword lists. Most clients see meaningful improvements in cost per lead and conversion rates within the first 60 days, with campaigns reaching full optimization around the 90-day mark.

Do you require long-term contracts?

No. We offer flexible month-to-month PPC management agreements. While we recommend a minimum 90-day engagement to properly optimize campaigns and gather enough data for statistically significant testing, you are never locked into a long-term contract you cannot exit. Our client retention rate speaks to the quality of our work — businesses stay because the results justify the investment, not because a contract forces them to.
